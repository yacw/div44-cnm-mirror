Administration

 
 

Heath Schechinger, Ph.D.: Co-chair

Heath is a licensed counseling psychologist at the University of California, Berkeley. In addition to serving as co-chair of the Consensual Non-monogamy Task Force, he has a private practice specializing in providing support to the consensual non-monogamy, kink, queer, and gender-nonconforming communities. He is also lead author of the largest study to date addressing therapy practices with consensual non-monogamy clients and his work has been featured in media outlets such as Vogue Australia, Goop, and Psychology Today.

 
 

Amy Moors, Ph.D.: Co-chair

Amy is an assistant professor of psychology at Chapman University and a research fellow at The Kinsey Institute, Indiana University. She has published more than 40 empirical articles and chapters related to consensual non-monogamies, gender, and sexuality as well as diversity and inclusion in higher education. Recently, she made Pacific Standard Magazine’s “30 Top Thinkers Under 30” list (however, sadly, became ineligible due to a recent birthday). She was recently recognized by the Society for the Scientific Study of Sexuality as a “Rising Scholar” for her early career contributions to the field.

 
 

Ashley Ramos: Executive Assistant

Ashley is a graduate student at Chapman University’s Marriage and Family Therapy Program. Her research interests include sexuality, pleasure, kink, and consensual non-monogamy. She plans to use her passion for sex education and therapy to create a sex-positive, kink-aware practice to serve marginalized communities.
